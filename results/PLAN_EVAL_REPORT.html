What Is Still At Risk

If this plan were executed as-is, the team would likely deliver a product that works end-to-end on the happy path but feels softer than the PRD in the places stakeholders will notice first. QA and product review would most likely flag generic or inconsistent AI behavior, underspecified concept-recommendation quality, and ambiguity around what happens to saved data during re-add, sync, or schema migration edge cases.

Remediation Direction

The remaining work is mostly specification hardening rather than architectural redesign. Tighten the plan by adding explicit acceptance criteria for AI tone, surface-specific context inputs, recommendation-quality gates, and CTA flows, then add concrete data-lifecycle rules for hidden statuses, re-add behavior, duplicate merges, and model-upgrade continuity so those edge cases are implemented intentionally instead of inferred during build-out.

  • Runtime, isolation, and destructive-test boundaries are concrete.
  • Core library persistence rules and autosave semantics are explicitly planned.
  • Ask and Show Detail both have clear structural contracts and key user journeys.
  • AI voice and discovery quality stay too principle-level in the submitted plan.
  • A few data-lifecycle edge cases remain underspecified: hidden Next, re-add, duplicate merges, and upgrade continuity.

Top Gaps And Why They Matter

The table in the markdown evaluation has 35 partial requirements. These five are the highest-leverage fixes because they either protect user data or define whether the app feels on-spec in stakeholder review.

critical

PRD-034 · Preserve saved libraries across data-model upgrades

The plan includes versioning and migrations, but without an explicit continuity/backfill path a schema change could strand or drop user libraries.

critical

PRD-026 · Re-add preserves My Data and refreshes public data

The plan covers merge behavior for saved shows, but it never explains how a removed show can be re-encountered without losing prior user overlay data.

important

PRD-091 · Validate discovery with rubric and hard-fail integrity

The plan references the quality bar, but without explicit gates the team could ship recommendations that feel okay in demos yet fail taste alignment or real-show integrity.

important

PRD-033 · Sync libraries/settings consistently and merge duplicates

The plan covers timestamps and persistence, but not explicit duplicate detection/merge flows, which risks silent data divergence across devices or sync events.

important

PRD-085 · Keep one consistent AI persona across surfaces

The plan uses shared AI infrastructure, but it does not turn cross-surface persona consistency into a concrete acceptance criterion, so the product voice could drift surface by surface.
