What this means

The core app shape is not the problem. The plan is already highly credible on runtime, navigation, detail screens, and export. The risk is that a few behavioral contracts still cut across multiple surfaces at once, which means drift can show up in both AI behavior and personalized data handling.

In practical terms: this is a buildable plan, but not yet a fully benchmark-hardened one.

What’s Strong

Areas that should give stakeholders confidence.

  • Runtime and isolation requirements are almost fully covered, including namespace scoping, user scoping, migrations, and benchmark-friendly auth injection.
  • Navigation, person detail, and settings/export all score at 100%, which means the non-AI shell of the product is well specified.
  • Show Detail and Ask are both high-coverage areas, so the plan already captures the product’s main engagement surfaces in substantial detail.
  • Collection save/remove defaults, timestamp fields, export shape, and data continuity are handled concretely rather than left as vague follow-up work.

What’s At Risk

The gaps do cluster, and the pattern matters.

  • AI Voice, Persona & Quality is the weakest area by a wide margin at 64.29%.
  • There are now four critical partials, and three of them sit directly on the product’s core promise: personalized overlays, AI actionability, and user-history preservation.
  • Recommendation quality is described, but not validated; there is no planned rubric or integrity hard-fail.
  • Several “exact semantics” items remain loose: hidden Next, Search-on-launch behavior, Search tone, detail-page order, duplicate merge handling, and transient provider-detail boundaries.

Top Gaps and Why They Matter

The issues most likely to show up first in QA, stakeholder review, or user trust.

Critical • PRD-086

Shared AI guardrails

The plan references guardrails, but not as a single reusable contract. Without that, spoiler policy, domain limits, and honesty standards can drift surface by surface.

Critical • PRD-018

Overlay everywhere

The plan states that the user's saved view should follow a show everywhere, but it only concretely wires badges and merged detail fetches. That leaves the central personalization promise under-specified outside key screens.

Critical • PRD-031

Real-show resolution

Ask has an explicit resolver path, but Explore Similar and Alchemy do not. That creates a real risk of dead-end recommendations in the app’s most differentiated discovery surfaces.

Critical • PRD-026

Re-add data preservation

The removal flow hard-deletes the show row, but the checklist also says re-adding should preserve prior My Data. Without a concrete retention model, that promise is not credible yet.

Important • PRD-091

Discovery validation rubric

There is no discovery-quality rubric or hard-fail integrity gate, so weak or hallucinated recommendations could ship without any explicit quality checkpoint.
